Gulab Jamun

Ingredients for Gulab Jamun Balls

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee or unsalted butter, melted
  • 3-4 tbsp milk (approximately)

Ingredients for Sugar Syrup

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)
  • 1 tsp rose water

Instructions

  • In a mixing bowl, combine the milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
  • Add the melted ghee and mix well until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  • Gradually add milk, little by little, and knead the mixture into a soft dough. The dough should be smooth and slightly sticky.
  • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
  • Meanwhile, prepare the sugar syrup by combining sugar, water, crushed cardamom pods, and saffron in a saucepan.
  • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 5-6 minutes until slightly thickened. Remove from heat and stir in the rose water. Keep the syrup warm.
  • Divide the dough into equal-sized portions and shape them into smooth balls without any cracks. Make sure the balls are compact and smooth, without any cracks.
  • Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan over medium-low heat. Once the ghee is hot, carefully add the dough balls in batches, without overcrowding the pan.
  • Fry the balls on low heat, stirring gently and occasionally, until they turn golden brown and evenly cooked from all sides. This may take about 8-10 minutes.
  • Once fried, remove the balls from the ghee using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil by placing them on paper towels.
  • Transfer the fried balls to the warm sugar syrup and let them soak for at least 1-2 hours, allowing them to absorb the syrup and become soft and spongy.
  • Serve Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts or dried rose petals, if desired.
  • Enjoy the deliciousness of homemade Gulab Jamun!
