Homemade Kimchi

Ingredients

  • 1 medium Napa cabbage
  • 1/4 cup sea salt
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 daikon radish, julienned
  • 4-6 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar

Instructions

  • Cut the Napa cabbage lengthwise into quarters and remove the core. Chop the cabbage into bite-sized pieces.
  • In a large bowl, dissolve the sea salt in water to create a brine. Add the chopped cabbage to the brine, making sure it's completely submerged. Let it sit for 1-2 hours.
  • Rinse the cabbage thoroughly under cold water to remove excess salt. Drain well and set aside.
  • In a separate bowl, mix together the julienned daikon radish, chopped green onions, Korean red pepper flakes, minced garlic, minced ginger, fish sauce (or soy sauce), and sugar.
  • Add the drained cabbage to the seasoning mixture. Use your hands to massage the seasoning into the cabbage, ensuring it's well coated.
  • Transfer the kimchi into a clean, airtight container. Press down firmly to remove any air pockets and ensure the cabbage is submerged in its liquid.
  • Cover the container loosely and let it ferment at room temperature for 1-2 days. Taste the kimchi periodically. Once it reaches the desired level of fermentation, transfer it to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process.
  • Enjoy your homemade kimchi as a side dish or in various recipes!
