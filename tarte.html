Tarte Tatin

Ingredients

  • 6-8 medium-sized apples (such as Granny Smith or Braeburn)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed if frozen
  • Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Peel the apples, cut them in half, and remove the cores.
  • In a 9 or 10-inch oven-safe skillet or Tarte Tatin pan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  • Add the sugar to the melted butter and cook, stirring occasionally, until the sugar is melted and turns golden brown to make a caramel sauce.
  • Arrange the apple halves, rounded side down, in the skillet on top of the caramel sauce, fitting them tightly together.
  • Cook the apples over medium heat for about 10-15 minutes until they are lightly caramelized and tender.
  • While the apples are cooking, roll out the puff pastry dough on a lightly floured surface to fit the size of the skillet.
  • Carefully place the puff pastry over the cooked apples, tucking in any excess dough around the edges.
  • Bake the Tarte Tatin in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy.
  • Remove the Tarte Tatin from the oven and let it cool in the skillet for about 5 minutes.
  • To serve, place a serving plate over the skillet and carefully invert the Tarte Tatin onto the plate so that the caramelized apples are on top.
  • Serve the Tarte Tatin warm, optionally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream on the side.
  • Enjoy your delicious homemade Tarte Tatin!
